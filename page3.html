My Five Biggest Stressors as an International Single Mother

The stress I face as a single mother isn't just about raising a child, it's about doing it all alone in a foreign country. As an international student, I have no family here. I left home to escape abuse, and just months after arriving, I lost my mother, my only support system. Now I juggle survival jobs, school, parenting, legal paperwork, and life with no help. These stressors are unique to women like me because we’re not just building a life, we’re rebuilding one from scratch.

1️⃣ Financial Pressure

Single mothers often face long term financial hardship and limited resources, which can lead to serious mental and emotional strain (CJCJ, 2017, p. 72).

My mother suffered under this kind of pressure. She worked endlessly to give us a better life, but the stress was never addressed. She developed a leg issue that led to chronic medication use, which damaged her liver. She passed away from it.

Now I carry that same burden. In Canada, I do multiple jobs braiding hair, cleaning, and cooking just to provide the basics for my daughter. Sometimes I skip my own meals or go without so she doesn’t have to.

2️⃣ Separation from My Child

Single mothers report high stress due to the sole responsibility of parenting, especially without emotional or practical support (CJCJ, 2017, p. 71).

I remember dropping her off at a friend’s house for childcare, watching her cry and beg me not to leave. I had no choice, I had to attend school or work because I am the only provider and caregiver. That kind of emotional pain lingers long after the door closes.

3️⃣ Lack of Support & Isolation

Single motherhood is isolating. When you’re caring for a child full time and trying to survive, there's no room left for a social life. I often drown myself in my schoolwork when my daughter is away just to avoid feeling the emptiness.

According to The Guardian (2024), loneliness is even more common among new mothers, especially those who are young, immigrants, without supportive partners, or raising kids with health challenges. I live that reality daily. When she’s not with me, I feel lost in a world that moves without me.

4️⃣ Behavioral Struggles in Children

Many single moms experience more parenting stress when their children show emotional or behavioral issues, especially when they can’t afford therapy or daycare (CJCJ, 2017, p. 74).

Growing up, my brother’s behavior was a major stressor for my mother. Without a male figure, it was hard for her to guide him. Now, I see the same pattern my daughter absorbs my stress. When I’m overwhelmed or distant, she becomes anxious or withdrawn.

Studies confirm this: children’s behavior problems can increase a mother’s stress, which then worsens the child’s behavior, a cycle that’s hard to break (Jiang et al., 2022).

5️⃣ Mental Health Strain (Anxiety & Depression)

The connection between parenting stress and depression is well established, especially in low income mothers without strong support systems (CJCJ, 2017, p. 76).

Research shows that single mothers are twice as likely to experience anxiety and depression compared to partnered moms (Liang et al., 2019).

In 2023, I hit a wall. I was chronically depressed. But I still had to be a mother. Still had to be a provider. The fear of the future eats away at me, especially when I don’t know how I’ll manage school, parenting, and life with so much uncertainty.
