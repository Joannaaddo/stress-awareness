Supportive Resources for Single Mothers

Page 6: Resources That Help Me

🆓 1. Free Resource: 211 Ontario

What it is:
A free, confidential helpline that connects people to local support services like housing, food banks, mental health, parenting help, and crisis hotlines.

Why it helps:
I can call 211 any time of day when I need info or help fast, like finding emergency childcare or food resources.

Visit: 211 Ontario
Call: 2-1-1 (available 24/7)

📍 2. Local Resource: Rexdale Women’s Centre (Toronto)

What it is:
A multicultural agency supporting immigrant women and children in Toronto with parenting help, legal referrals, housing assistance, ESL, and crisis counselling.

Why it helps:
If you are a newcomer like me, this is a safe place to ask for help without judgment. They even offer programs for single moms.

Visit: Rexdale Women’s Centre
Location: 925 Albion Rd., Etobicoke, ON

🧠 3. Mental Health Resource: BounceBack Ontario

What it is:
A free skill-building program for stress, anxiety, and low mood — available online or by phone, with a personal coach.

Why it helps:
As a mom and student, I cannot always get to therapy. This lets me build coping skills on my schedule.

Visit: BounceBack Ontario

👩🏾‍💼 4. Student-Mom Focused: Centennial College Student Services

What it is:
Centennial College offers free supports for student parents:

  • Counselling
  • Academic advising
  • Emergency aid
  • Food bank
  • Health services
  • Financial aid and OSAP help

Why it helps:
Being a student and a mom means I carry a double load. Knowing there is support on campus keeps me going.

Visit: Student Services
Ask in person: At your campus’s Student Hub
